Biowizard 2012

Biowizard, the Annual Biotechnology Quiz competition for the Higher Secondary students, was started in the year 2010. It was first hosted in conjunction with the Biovision, the International Symposia conducted every year by the Biotechnology Engineering Department of the Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology. In its maiden voyage Biowizard was able to attract students from over 50 higher secondary schools. Biovison, Covers various aspects of the field of biotechnology like General Biotechnology, Environmental Biotechnology, Food Biotechnology, Marine Biotechnology and Bioprocess Engineering to name a few. Biovision has always aimed at exposing students to the challenges in the field of biotechnology. Students and delegates from all over India and abroad have honoured this symposium with their dignified presence. Biowizard was born out of the intention of sparking the interests of the higher secondary students in the area of biotechnology. Biotechnology is an emerging field with a huge potential. Many are yet ignorant as to the vast opportunities this field promises. Biowizard was conducted to help the students interested in biotechnology to make the right choice. It could be said as their introduction to the world of biotechnology beyond their textbooks. The only eligibility of attending the quiz is to be a higher secondary student in the biology stream with a basic knowledge of Biomolecules, Cell Biology, Immunology, Ecology, Biotechnology, Enzymology, Microbes, and Genetics. The subjects are all covered in their regular curriculum. Schools from all over Kerala are invited. On- the-spot registration is also made possible for students who might have been unable to send the registration forms on time. One school can register three teams consisting of two students each. A nominal fee of Rs.50 is charged as the registration fee. Over 70 teams from over 50 schools participated last year most of them with maximum allowed participation. The quiz consists of five rounds; namely factual round, visual round, choose your stream round, linked round and rapid fire round. After the final round the winners are announced and they are given the Biowizard Title. The title is accompanied by a cash prize of Rs 2000 and an ever rolling trophy. The runners up are given a cash award of Rs 1000.

The outstanding discoveries of genetic composition of organisms and the structure and function of the genes and total genome analysis of man gave a great boost to biotechnology in recent years. The transfer of genetic material from one living organism to the other and its continued ability to function in the same manner as it was in the donor gave rise to the technology of genetic engineering. The production of insulin, stomatostatin and other used products for correction and prevention of human diseases; the production of monoclonal antibodies for effective treatment of serious disease like cancer is other achievement.

The impacts of biotechnology in industry, agriculture, animal husbandry, pharmaceutical products, medicine, and environmental management have been beyond comprehension and the future result will be beyond the imagination of mankind. Even production of spare human parts through embro-stem culture is on the anvil. The world market during 2000 AD was 8000 million ECUs and the edible vaccine is expected to have a market of 40 billion after 5 years.

India has not made desired contribution in this important field of biotechnology, which is going to be the major foreign exchange earner in the world. In this context we cannot lag behind; in promoting education, research/contract research and extension activities to catch up with the progress of the rest of the world.

Considering all these aspects, Sahrdaya College of Engineering and Technology, has decided to initiate teaching programmes in, Biotechnology and the first 3 batches of B-Tech students have already been admitted. The college has an ambitious programme to give a lead to biotechnology as a major source of providing human resource and promoting industry in earning foreign exchange and meeting the indigenous market in an effective manner. Along with this the programmes in biotechnology are formulated to serve the society through people's participation providing its benefits to the rural population.
